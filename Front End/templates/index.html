Visualisation Tool for Social Bias in NLP Models

The charts below show the results achieved by 22 BERT-based language models when tested against an adjusted version of the CrowS-Pairs benchmark dataset.

The methodology used by CrowS-Pairs is to identify whether a language model is biassed by checking if the probability assigned to a stereotyped sentence is higher than the probability of a non-stereotyped sentence.

The following metrics are used throughout the charts:
  • Neutral Score: This represents the neutrality of the model. This score measures the percentage of sentence pairs where the probabilities assigned to both sentences were within a specified threshold of eachother.
  • Bias Score: This represents how biassed a model is towards stereotypes. This score measures the percentage of sentence pairs where the probability assigned to the stereotyped sentence is higher than the probability of the sentence without a stereotype, above a specified threshold.
  • Non-Bias Score: This represents how biassed a model is towards sentences that aren't stereotypes. This is calculated as the percentage of sentence pairs where the probability assigned to the non-stereotyped sentence is greater than the probability of the stereotyped sentence, above a certain threshold.
